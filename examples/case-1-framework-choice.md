# Case 1 — Framework Choice

**Question:** "I'm a frontend beginner. Should I learn React or Vue first?"

*Type: Decision / Advice. Feels easy. Has a well-known "standard answer." High autopilot risk.*

---

## Without Just Do More

> I'd recommend starting with **Vue (especially Vue 3)**. Here's why:
>
> - **Gentler learning curve**: Vue's template syntax is closer to HTML. Beginners are less likely to hit a wall early.
> - **Excellent documentation**: Vue's docs are widely considered among the best in the frontend ecosystem.
> - **Clear conceptual separation**: Data, template, and logic are separated in a way that makes frontend concepts intuitive to learn.
>
> That said, consider **React first** if:
> - React jobs are significantly more common in your target city (worth checking job boards)
> - You plan to do React Native mobile development
> - You already have solid JavaScript fundamentals and aren't put off by JSX
>
> **A practical tip:** Search for frontend roles in your target city and see which framework appears more often. In larger metro areas React often leads; in other markets Vue may be more prevalent.
>
> One more thing: the core concepts are transferable. Once you know one framework well, the other takes weeks, not months, to learn. Don't agonize over this too long — pick one and start.

---

## With Just Do More

> Direct answer first, no hedging.
>
> **Start with Vue.** Not because Vue is "better" — because of your specific situation as a beginner.
>
> ---
>
> **Why Vue for a beginner**
>
> **1. Lower frustration in the first month**
> Vue's template syntax is close to HTML you already know. React's first barrier is JSX plus a purely functional thinking model — two new things at once. For a beginner, absorbing both simultaneously leads to getting stuck and quitting.
>
> **2. The ecosystem fits where beginners actually search for help**
> Vue was created by Evan You, who built it with developer experience as a core goal. The documentation, tutorials, and community Q&A are unusually high quality. When you hit a wall — and you will — finding answers quickly matters more than the framework itself.
>
> **3. You lose nothing career-wise**
> If you later want to work at a company that uses React heavily, learning Vue first doesn't hurt. The concepts transfer: components, reactive state, lifecycle, props — once you understand these in Vue, React is a 2-4 week adjustment, not a restart.
>
> ---
>
> **When React is the right first choice:**
> - Your specific target company uses React (check their job postings, not generic job boards)
> - You have a mentor or team you can ask directly, and they use React
> - You already write JavaScript comfortably and want the wider ecosystem from day one
>
> ---
>
> **The meta-point that matters more than the framework choice:**
>
> The actual risk here isn't choosing the wrong framework. It's using the comparison as a reason not to start. The question "React or Vue?" can become a research loop — reading comparisons, watching videos, never writing a component.
>
> Pick Vue. Start today. Two weeks in, you'll realize the framework debate is nearly irrelevant for beginners.

## What Changed

**Real Intent** revealed the real question. "React or Vue?" is not asking for a framework comparison. It's asking: *which one should I start today so my first three months aren't wasted?* That reframe changed the entire answer.

The baseline was technically sound — it gave accurate, balanced information. But it ended with "don't agonize too long, just start" without naming *why that's the most important thing to say*. The skill version led with the procrastination trap because Real Intent identified it as the actual problem: the comparison question itself is often a form of delay.

**Blind Spot** caught a context assumption. The questioner is a beginner looking for an unambiguous recommendation, not a balanced analysis. The baseline gave balance. The skill version gave a decision — which is what a beginner actually needs.

**The baseline gave a good answer. The skill gave the right answer for this person.**
