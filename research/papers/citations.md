# Paper Citations

These papers informed the design of Just Do More.

## Kojima et al., 2022

**Large Language Models are Zero-Shot Reasoners**

Takeshi Kojima, Shixiang Shane Gu, Machel Reid, Yutaka Matsuo, Yusuke Iwasawa

*Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2022*

arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11916

Why it matters here: it is the clearest early demonstration that a small reasoning cue before the answer can materially improve performance on reasoning tasks.

## Wang et al., 2023

**Plan-and-Solve Prompting: Improving Zero-Shot Chain-of-Thought Reasoning by Large Language Models**

Lei Wang, Wanyu Xu, Yihuai Lan, Zhiqiang Hu, Yunshi Lan, Roy Ka-Wei Lee, Ee-Peng Lim

*Proceedings of the 61st Annual Meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL), 2023*

arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.04091

Why it matters here: it supports separating planning from execution, which is close to the role played by the three silent checks.

## Yang et al., 2023

**Large Language Models as Optimizers**

Chengrun Yang, Xuezhi Wang, Yifeng Lu, Hanxiao Liu, Quoc V. Le, Denny Zhou, Xinyun Chen

arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.03409

Why it matters here: it supports the idea that instruction framing and metacognitive cues can change answer quality.
